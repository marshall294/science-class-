Adaptation and Variation

Understanding how life evolves and survives in changing environments

How Organisms Are Adapted to Their Environments

Adaptation refers to the special characteristics that organisms develop to survive in their specific environments. These traits can be physical, behavioral, or physiological.

  • Physical adaptations: Thick fur in polar bears to withstand cold climates.
  • Behavioral adaptations: Migration of birds to escape harsh winters.
  • Physiological adaptations: Cacti storing water in arid environments.

Evolution and Adaptation

Evolution is the process through which organisms change over time to better adapt to their environments. This occurs through genetic variations that provide certain advantages.

What Is Variation?

Variation refers to the differences between individuals of the same species. These differences may be due to genetics or environmental factors.

Importance of Variation:

  • Ensures survival by providing adaptability to changes in the environment.
  • Drives the process of natural selection.

What Is Natural Selection?

Natural selection is the process where organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those traits to the next generation. This concept was introduced by Charles Darwin.

"Survival of the fittest" - Charles Darwin

Causes of Extinction

Extinction occurs when a species is unable to adapt to environmental changes or compete effectively for resources. Causes include:

  • Climate change
  • Habitat loss
  • Overhunting or overfishing
  • Introduction of invasive species
