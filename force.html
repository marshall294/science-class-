Understanding Resultant Force and Graphs

What is Resultant Force?

The resultant force is the single force that has the same effect as two or more forces acting together. It is the vector sum of all forces acting on an object.

Formula: If two forces, F1 and F2, are acting in the same direction:

Resultant Force (FR) = F1 + F2

If the forces are acting in opposite directions:

FR = |F1 - F2|

Example

If a force of 10 N and another force of 15 N are acting in the same direction, the resultant force is:

FR = 10 N + 15 N = 25 N

If the forces are acting in opposite directions:

FR = |10 N - 15 N| = 5 N

Understanding Resultant Force with Graphs

Example 1:

Two forces \( F_1 = 10 \, \text{N} \) and \( F_2 = 15 \, \text{N} \) act at an angle of \( 60^\circ \). Find the magnitude and direction of the resultant force.

Solution:

Using the formula:

\[ R = \sqrt{F_1^2 + F_2^2 + 2 \cdot F_1 \cdot F_2 \cdot \cos(\theta)} \]

Substitute \( F_1 = 10 \, \text{N} \), \( F_2 = 15 \, \text{N} \), and \( \theta = 60^\circ \):

\[ R = \sqrt{10^2 + 15^2 + 2 \cdot 10 \cdot 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)} \]

\[ R = \sqrt{100 + 225 + 150 \times 0.5} \]

\[ R = \sqrt{475} \approx 21.79 \, \text{N} \]

Direction \( \alpha \):

\[ \tan(\alpha) = \frac{F_{2y}}{F_{1x} + F_{2x}}, \, \alpha = 36.68^\circ \]

Example 2:

Forces \( F_1 = 8 \, \text{N} \), \( F_2 = 12 \, \text{N} \), and angle \( \theta = 45^\circ \).

\[ R = \sqrt{8^2 + 12^2 + 2 \cdot 8 \cdot 12 \cdot \cos(45^\circ)} \]

Solution: \( R \approx 17.03 \, \text{N} \), Direction: \( \alpha \approx 28.14^\circ \).

Example 3:

Forces \( F_1 = 20 \, \text{N} \), \( F_2 = 25 \, \text{N} \), and angle \( \theta = 30^\circ \).

\[ R = \sqrt{20^2 + 25^2 + 2 \cdot 20 \cdot 25 \cdot \cos(30^\circ)} \]

Solution: \( R \approx 42.72 \, \text{N} \), Direction: \( \alpha \approx 33.69^\circ \).

Example 4:

Forces \( F_1 = 5 \, \text{N} \), \( F_2 = 10 \, \text{N} \), and angle \( \theta = 90^\circ \).

\[ R = \sqrt{5^2 + 10^2} = 11.18 \, \text{N} \]

Direction: \( \alpha = 63.43^\circ \).

Example 5:

Forces \( F_1 = 30 \, \text{N} \), \( F_2 = 35 \, \text{N} \), and angle \( \theta = 120^\circ \).

\[ R = \sqrt{30^2 + 35^2 + 2 \cdot 30 \cdot 35 \cdot \cos(120^\circ)} \]

Solution: \( R \approx 39.05 \, \text{N} \), Direction: \( \alpha \approx 65.37^\circ \).
