Year 5 Math: Word Problems in Addition & Subtraction

Definition

Word problems in addition and subtraction involve real-life scenarios where you must determine whether to add or subtract to find the correct answer.

Step-by-Step Instructions

How to solve word problems:

  • Read the problem carefully.
  • Identify the key information (numbers and operations).
  • Determine if the problem requires addition or subtraction.
  • Solve step by step.
  • Check your answer to ensure it makes sense.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Sarah has 125 candies. She buys 75 more. How many does she have in total?

Solution: 125 + 75 = 200

Example 2: A farmer has 342 cows. He sells 148. How many cows does he have left?

Solution: 342 - 148 = 194

Classwork

1. Jane has 250 books. She buys 135 more. How many does she have now?

2. A school has 987 students. 264 graduate. How many are left?

3. Tom earns $450 and spends $275. How much does he have left?

4. A shop sells 129 chocolates in the morning and 187 in the afternoon. How many chocolates were sold in total?

5. A garden has 625 flowers. 298 are picked. How many remain?

6. A train has 432 passengers. 167 get off. How many are left?

Assignments

1. A bakery makes 350 loaves of bread in the morning and 275 in the evening. How many loaves are made in total?

2. A factory produces 1,200 toys. 567 are sold. How many are left?

3. A library has 5,000 books. 2,375 are borrowed. How many are still on the shelves?

4. A farmer collects 879 eggs on Monday and 765 on Tuesday. How many eggs were collected in total?

5. A company has 3,450 employees. 1,678 leave. How many employees remain?
